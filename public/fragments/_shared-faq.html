How much does UX/UI design cost?

The cost varies depending on the project scope, interface complexity, number of screens, and unique requirements. Usually, a professional studio will provide a custom quote after an initial assessment. At Triolla, average UX Design varies from $15K-$40K 

How long does the UX/UI design process take?

The duration depends on the project scope, number of screens, and complexity. Typically, UX/UI design for a complex platform or app takes from 6 weeks to 3-4 months. Proper planning, client collaboration, and timely provision of materials speed up the process and ensure a high-quality result.

What does the workflow with a UX/UI studio include?

The workflow with a UX/UI Triolla studio begins with an introductory meeting to understand the business and user needs. Next comes user experience research, concept design, interface design, prototyping, and usability testing. Triolla supports the client all the way through to delivering development-ready files, maintaining full collaboration throughout the process.

What’s the difference between UX design and UI design?

UX design focuses on the user’s journey, system structure, and flow. UI (User Interface) design deals with the visual appearance of the interface—colors, typography, icons, and graphic elements. The right combination of UX and UI creates a product that is convenient, clear, and visually appealing.

What are the main stages in a UX/UI design project?

At Triolla, the main stages are: research and discovery, wireframing, concept design, interface (UI) design, building an interactive prototype, usability testing, revisions and improvements, and preparing files for development. Each stage is carried out in collaboration with the client, focusing on business and user goals.

What does the user research process look like in a UX project?

User research is a pivotal stage at any process of product design in Triolla. It includes target audience analysis, persona definition, mapping user journeys, and understanding user needs and pain points. This stage is critical for creating an accurate and tailored user experience and forms the basis for all design and development processes.
